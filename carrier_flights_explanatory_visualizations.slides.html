Effects of Flight Characteristics on Their Probability of Being Cancelled, Diverted or Delayed¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation, I wanted to look at the characteristics of flights that could be used to predict their flight status. The main focus was on different carriers, different airports, distance of the flight, airtime, and day of the week.

Dataset Overview¶

The data consisted of flight_status and attributes of approximately 2.4 million US flights in 2008. The attributes included delay compared to estimated arrival and departure, as well as additional measurements such as flight distance, origin and destination airport, date, and day of the week.

Distribution of Flight Statuses¶

Flights in this dataset are mostly On Time/Early or Delayed, with 1,734,538 falling into the former category and 584,883 falling into the latter. Cancelled and Diverted flights are much less common in this dataset respectively: 64,442 and 5,654.

Flight Status by Carrier¶

Each indiviudal carrier varies greatly in terms of its individual breakdown of Cancelled, Diverted, Delayed, and On Time/Early flights. For example, the carrier EV has more cancelled flights than any other type of flight, and carriers XE, HA, MQ, AQ, AS, B6, AA, AS and NW have more Delayed than On Time/Early flights. This may be one of the most significant factors in determining whether a flight will be on time or not

Conclusions¶

My biggest takeaway from this project is that carrier probably the most influential choice you have to determine whether or not your flight might get delayed, diverted, or cancelled. Some carriers, like WN have a great track record of getting the plane in on time, while others are less dependable. Other considerations, are flight distance, wherein long flights are slightly more likely to be diverted, and airport size, wherein large airports are more consistent in cancellations and small airports can vary quite a bit. Since distance distribution is normal based on a log scale, it may be hard to find a wide range of carriers for very long distance flights, since there are less of those flights offered in general
